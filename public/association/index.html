Crays Association

The Crays operating layer.

Your Crays operating layer is a community-operated brand organized through a Swiss association. You use it to coordinate brand access, standards, project approvals and DAO-ready participation without turning independent ecosystem ventures into association-managed businesses.

The Association gives you the legal body that can register and protect the Crays brand globally. It gives the DAO a lawful home for brand and community decisions while every ecosystem company keeps its own management and economic decisions.

Membership and participation

Access, contribution, voting and investment stay separate.

When you join, you are assigned to a group. Your group defines access, rank and voting weight while economic participation stays outside the Association.

50%

Community Group

If you join through a Nostr Client badge, you vote inside the Community Group.

25%

Core Group

If you founded a Crays company or serve in Steering or Extended Steering, you carry Core Group responsibility.

25%

Investor Group

If you invest in Crays ecosystem companies, infrastructure, local entities or approved project structures, you vote through the Investors Group.

0%

DAO visibility

If you only use a product once, you can see the DAO without voting rights until you become badge-verified.

Access

You can receive practical benefits

You can receive reduced-rate services, events, training, club offers, partner access and ecosystem services.

Proof

Your Nostr badge can prove voting rank

Your Nostr Client badge can represent your Community Group status and support electronic voting through the DAO platform.

Duties

You help protect the system

You follow the governance rules, decisions, fees, ecosystem standards and legal requirements attached to your role.

Changes

Group assignment is kept current

If your role changes, the General Secretariat must be informed so your group assignment and voting logic stay accurate.

From interest to execution

Join, vote, approve, execute.

Important decisions stay inside a formal member and Council process, while specialist teams and dedicated entities execute. The goal is clarity before people commit time, money, locations or reputation.

1

Apply or propose

A person, partner, operator, investor or member brings an application, project idea, group matter or ecosystem proposal into the process.

2

Assign the right role

The General Secretariat and Council logic clarify membership type, member group, partner status, project route and required approvals.

3

Prepare the decision

Agendas, documents, budgets, member register, project terms and implementation routes are prepared before formal decisions are made.

4

Vote where required

Member Assembly, Groups Assembly or Council decide depending on competence. Standard matters can use simple majority; major matters need stronger support.

5

Execute through the right team

General Secretariat, executive teams, local entities, project companies or partners implement under Crays standards and compliance rules.

6

Monitor and improve

The association monitors standards, member rights, brand use, local profit centers, compliance and ecosystem quality over time.

Guardrails

Rules protect the community, brand and partners.

For a global network, trust is a product feature. The governance rules define clear bodies, voting weights, majorities, fund use, dispute handling and limits around profit distribution.

Purpose

Funds stay tied to the mission

Association funds are used transparently for association purposes, infrastructure, standards, IT, club rules with DAO proof.

Voting

No single interest group owns the system

Community, Core and Investors carry weighted voting shares of 50/25/25 while visibility access stays outside voting.

Majorities

Sensitive changes need broader support

Statute changes, purpose changes, seat transfer, Council or Auditor changes and dissolution require a qualified majority.

Compliance

Local clubs must follow standards

The association can support and monitor Crays Clubs worldwide to enforce Crays standards and compliance requirements.

Disputes

Conflicts start with settlement

Disputes are designed to go through settlement first, with the option of digital or AI-supported negotiation before court routes in Zug.
